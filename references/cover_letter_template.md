# Cover Letter Tone Guide

## Voice
- Confident, direct, and pragmatic. Sound like an engineer ready to ship code.
- Discuss architecture (like LLM orchestration or client-server design) without overwhelming jargon. Focus on the why and the impact.
- Show genuine curiosity about the specific scaling, product, or systems challenges their engineering team is actively tackling.

## What to Emphasize
- Highlight the ability to take a project from concept to deployment (e.g., seamlessly bridging a backend API with a polished frontend interface).
- LLM/agent experience as a differentiator, Use of LLM for rapid hackathon prototype
- Briefly mention why a specific technology was used to solve a problem (e.g., leveraging Go for backend performance or Python/FastAPI for rapid AI integration).

## What to Avoid
- Ban phrases like "I am excited to apply" or "I believe my coursework makes me a great fit." Let your independent builds speak for your competence.
- Do not list out languages or frameworks. Show them in action within a sentence.
- Cut "passionate," "driven," and "hardworking." Replace them with concrete evidence of those traits.
- Over-formal language ("Herewith I submit my application")

## Preferred Openers (pick style, not exact words)
- Lead with a specific, technical observation about their product, platform, or recent release.
- Lead with a direct result from a recent, highly relevant build that mirrors their stack or mission.

## Preferred Closers
- "I’d welcome the chance to discuss how my experience building scalable platforms can contribute to your team."
- Avoid: "Thank you for your time and consideration" (too passive)

## Length
- Max 250 words
- 3 paragraphs only
- Short sentences preferred
